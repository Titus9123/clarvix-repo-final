Public Revenue Leak Scan↑

What it checks

Public website speed and mobile path; CTA clarity; form, phone and WhatsApp paths; visible tracking tags and pixel signals; landing-message consistency.

What it tries to find

Lead leaks that can be seen without access: broken forms, weak calls to action, missing click paths, public tracking gaps and friction that can reduce enquiries.

What you get

A prioritized list of public revenue leaks and the safest next step. No passwords, no account access, no private data.

Revenue Leak Verify Lite↑

What it checks

GA4, Google Search Console and GTM in read-only mode; conversion events; traffic-to-lead gaps; search visibility signals; tag/container consistency.

What it tries to find

Which public suspicions are real, which are false alarms, and which need more data before spending money on fixes.

What you get

A confirmed-vs-suspected leak report and an implementation plan that can be approved later.

Revenue Leak Monitor↑

What it checks

Recurring checks for tracking breaks, lead path drops, campaign signal problems, form/CTA issues and SEO/traffic anomalies.

What it tries to find

New leaks before they silently cost leads: tracking regressions, sudden drops, weak pages, wasted-spend signals and unresolved action items.

What you get

Ongoing AI monitoring, alerts, prioritization and monthly business-owner summaries.

AdTech Control Tower↑

What it checks

Everything in Monitor plus an approved action backlog, implementation readiness, verification tasks and executive control reporting.

What it tries to achieve

Move from diagnosis to controlled improvement without blind automation: every proposed change has a reason, approval gate and rollback/verification path.

What you get

A control tower for approved fixes, weekly executive briefs and a clear audit trail of what changed and why.
